Site Plan: Global Trade Center (GTC)

Prepared by: Francisca Maxinho

Site Name

Name: Global Trade Center (GTC)

Reason for Selection: The name "Global Trade Center" clearly states the portal's authority and institutional nature. Utilizing the acronym "GTC" allows for strong, memorable corporate branding, clean logo spacing, and instantly signals a centralized hub for small businesses seeking international market entry.

Site Purpose

The Global Trade Center (GTC) acts as an Export Readiness Portal designed to help small and medium enterprises (SMEs) prepare for international trade and exporting. Many small businesses possess high export potential but struggle due to a lack of centralized, beginner-friendly resources to evaluate their operations and discover structured pathways[cite: 12]. GTC bridges this structural gap [cite: 13] by combining three key content spaces into a multi-page framework consisting of a Home page, a Resources page, and a Quiz page[cite: 10]:

  • ITC Training Modules: Integrates detailed guidance and external links to training resources from the International Trade Centre's SME Trade Academy[cite: 6].
  • Access2Markets Guidance: Provides critical navigation tutorials for the European Union's Access2Markets engine to help firms uncover tariff details, rules of origin, and compliance rules[cite: 7].
  • Export Readiness Quiz: A dynamic 60-question self-assessment tracking business preparedness across finance, logistics, compliance, operations, and market intelligence [cite: 8], generating diagnostic final scores and immediate strategic feedback[cite: 9].

Target Audience Scenarios

The layout and feature priorities of the GTC portal are guided by addressing these key target user questions:

1. "I am an SME owner looking to export to the European Union for the first time. Where can I quickly find out what tariffs or regulatory entry requirements apply to my specific product category?"

Portal Response: The user can navigate to the dedicated Resources Page [cite: 10], which features structured guidance and interactive pathways linking straight to the EU's Access2Markets database fields[cite: 7].

2. "I believe my company is operationally strong, but I lack an official audit of our systemic risks. How can I evaluate our structural gaps across legal compliance and export logistics?"

Portal Response: The user can access the Export Readiness Quiz from the primary navigation bar [cite: 10] and take the comprehensive 60-question evaluation [cite: 8] to get localized scores and customized logistical recommendations[cite: 9].

3. "Where can I find free, reputable online training documentation to educate my logistics team on foreign market research and basic export paperwork?"

Portal Response: The Resources Page features a dedicated section breaking down available training paths from the International Trade Centre (ITC) SME Trade Academy[cite: 6, 10].

Color Scheme

To establish a highly authoritative visual identity and secure a 100% compliant color contrast pass under WebAIM AA accessibility guidelines, the following color variables are defined and applied throughout this site plan:

  • Deep Navy Blue (#1a365d): Primary branding color. Applied to main page banners, primary titles, and structural header containers to express corporate reliability and security.
  • Steel Blue (#2b6cb0): Secondary palette color. Applied to subtitles, content section titles, and semantic iconography to establish structural text hierarchy.
  • Amber Gold (#b7791f): Accent performance color (optimized for a safe text/background contrast ratio). Applied to highlighted elements, active borders, and primary interactive links.
  • Charcoal Gray (#2d3748): Dominant typography color. Applied to all paragraph strings and list elements to optimize legibility and protect user eyesight from screen strain.
  • Light Slate (#f7fafc): Underlying canvas color. Used for page backgrounds to provide contrast against white content blocks.

Typography

Clean, high-performance typography ensures optimal reading speeds on mobile devices:

  • Font Family: "Helvetica Neue", Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif.
  • Application: Arial and clean sans-serif alternatives are universally applied to headers, forms, navigation tools, and paragraphs. Primary section headers use a bold weight ($700$) at $1.75\text{rem}$ to establish strong section boundaries, while standard text layers utilize a weight of $400$ paired with a spacious line-height ($1.6$) to maximize visual clarity.
